History and Other Facts

Daniel Rutherford was the first to consider Nitrogen as an element, but he was not the first one to actually use the element or discover it. The earliest form of Nitrogen was found in Egypt by “alchemist” in the form of “Sal Ammonia,” and this was found by the alchemist heating dung, urine, and salt. “Pure” Nitrogen Gas was founded by two chemist in the 1760s by the name of Henry Cavendish, and Joseph Priestley.They removed the Oxygen in the air in a controlled environment. The two conducted experiments with the newly found gas, some of their most notable findings were that it could extinguish the flame of a candle, and a mouse that had breathed it in, would die shortly after. Daniel Rutherford was the one in September of 1772, in his doctoral thesis, at Edinburgh, Scotland. Rutherford is now credited with the founding of the Element known as Nitrogen.
